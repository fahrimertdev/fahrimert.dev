# Hello World

Every builder starts somewhere. This is my somewhere.

I've been building things on the internet for a while — mostly freelance projects for clients, tools that scratch my own itch, experiments that go nowhere. But I've never really shared the process. That changes now.

## Why build in public?

A few reasons:

**Accountability.** If I write about what I'm building, I'm more likely to actually ship it. Deadlines are fake when they're only in your head.

**Learning.** Writing forces clarity. If I can't explain what I'm building in one sentence, I probably don't understand it well enough.

**Community.** The indie hacker community is genuinely one of the best corners of the internet. Builders helping builders.

## What I'm working on

Right now my focus is **Previewly** — a tool that makes it easier for freelancers to share deploy previews with clients and collect feedback. No more "can you scroll down a bit and click the button" in a Zoom call. Clients get a link, they comment directly on the page, they approve. Done.

It's in early building stage. More updates coming soon.

## What to expect here

This blog is going to be raw. Build logs, technical write-ups, occasional rants, lessons learned. Not polished content marketing — just honest documentation of the journey.

If you're building something too, I'd love to hear about it. Say hi on [Twitter](https://x.com/fahrimert).

Let's ship things.
